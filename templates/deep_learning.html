What is Deep Learning?

Deep learning is a subdivision of machine learning that can be described as a neural network consisting of three or more layers.1 While traditional neural networks typically have only one or two hidden layers, deep learning architectures go deeper by incorporating three or more hidden layers, hence the term "deep."

Some Application of Deep Learning

Deep learning, a subfield of artificial intelligence (AI), has revolutionized various industries by providing powerful tools for solving complex problems. With its ability to analyze large amounts of data and extract meaningful patterns, deep learning has found applications in diverse domains. Here are some notable applications of deep learning:3

  • Visual Recognition

    Deep learning models possess the ability to acquire intricate data representations, allowing them to excel in tasks such as image classification, object detection, and facial recognition, surpassing previous benchmarks in performance.

  • Fraud Detection

    Deep learning offers various avenues for fraud detection, including training models to recognize established fraud patterns. This involves providing the model with a dataset containing documented instances of fraud, enabling it to identify similar patterns in new cases and flag them accordingly. This application stands out as one of the most effective uses of deep learning.

Fraud Detection using Deep Learning

Fraud detection is a critical task in various industries, including finance, insurance, and e-commerce, where identifying and preventing fraudulent activities is of utmost importance. Deep learning, a subset of machine learning, has emerged as a powerful technique for detecting fraud due to its ability to automatically learn complex patterns and representations from large amounts of data.By leveraging deep learning algorithms, organizations can enhance their fraud detection capabilities and minimize financial losses, protect customer data, and maintain trust in their systems. Recognizing fraudulent credit card transactions is crucial for credit card companies to ensure that customers are not wrongfully charged for unauthorized purchases.4

  • Deep Neural Network Model

    A deep neural network model, also known as a deep learning model, is a type of artificial neural network that consists of multiple layers of interconnected nodes, called neurons. It is designed to mimic the structure and functioning of the human brain's neural networks.

    Deep neural networks have been widely utilized in fraud detection systems due to their ability to learn complex patterns and extract meaningful features from large-scale datasets.


    • About the Datasets

    Obtaining publicly available datasets in the field of financial services, particularly in the emerging mobile money transactions domain, poses a challenge. These datasets are highly valuable to researchers, especially those focused on fraud detection. The scarcity of such datasets can be attributed to the inherently private nature of financial transactions, making it difficult to find publicly accessible datasets in this domain. [5].

    To address this issue, the use of a simulator called PaySim to generate synthetic datasets. PaySim leverages aggregated data from private datasets to create a synthetic dataset that closely simulates the typical transaction operations. Additionally, it introduces malicious behaviors to assess the effectiveness of various fraud detection methods.[5].


    Datasets Description[6].

    Datasets play a crucial role in fraud detection by providing the foundation for developing and evaluating effective fraud detection algorithms. These datasets encompass the following description:

    • type

      CASH-IN, CASH-OUT, DEBIT, PAYMENT and TRANSFER.

    • amount

      amount of the transaction in local currency.

    • oldbalanceOrg

      initial balance before the transaction

    • newbalanceOrig

      new balance after the transaction

    • oldbalanceDest

      Old balance recipient before the transaction. Note that there is not information for customers that start with M (Merchants).

    • newbalanceDest

      initial balance recipient before the transaction. Note that there is not information for customers that start with M (Merchants).
