What is Neural Network?

Neural networks, known as artificial neural networks (ANNs) or simulated neural networks (SNNs), are a key component of deep learning algorithms that emulate the communication patterns of neurons in the human brain.[1]

Common Activition
Sigmoid

The sigmoid activation function transforms the weighted sum into a numerical value ranging from 0 to 1.[2] This function is called "sigmoid" because it resembles an S-shaped curve. By applying this function, the neural network can introduce non-linearity to its outputs, enabling it to capture complex relationships and make predictions with a probabilistic interpretation.

ReLu Activiation Function

The rectified linear unit (ReLU) activation function, in comparison to smooth functions like the sigmoid, is often found to be more effective in neural networks due to its simpler computational requirements and a wider range of responsiveness, as supported by empirical evidence. Unlike the sigmoid function, which rapidly diminishes its responsiveness on both ends, ReLU maintains a more useful and consistent level of responsiveness.[2]

PCOS Diagnosis using Neural Network

Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS) is a condition that impacts women in their reproductive years, characterized by hormonal imbalances and metabolic issues, which can have implications for their overall well-being.[3]

Diagnosis of PCOS typically involves a combination of evaluating symptoms, medical history, physical examination, and laboratory tests. While there is no cure for PCOS, the management focuses on alleviating symptoms and minimizing potential health risks. Treatment approaches may include lifestyle modifications, such as adopting a healthy diet and engaging in regular physical activity, as well as medications to regulate menstrual cycles, manage hormonal imbalances, and address specific symptoms or health concerns.

There are applications of neural networks in the context of PCOS due to the complex and multifaceted nature of the condition. Neural networks have the ability to analyze and interpret large amounts of data, identify patterns, and make predictions based on these patterns. In the case of PCOS, neural networks can be utilized to aid in various aspects such as diagnosis, risk prediction, and treatment optimization.

FeedForward Neural Network

A feedforward neural network can be viewed as a nonlinear function that converts input variables into output variables.[5].

Feedforward refers to the flow of information in a neural network, where data travels through the network in one direction, from the input layer to the output layer, without any loops or feedback connections. In a feedforward neural network, the information moves in a forward direction, passing through the layers of the network in a sequential manner.

To predict the PCOS diagnosis in this application, we utilize a Feedforward Neural Network Model.
